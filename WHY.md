Velanza — AI-driven lexical engineering in the spirit of PostGPT.
This project uses an adversarial system with two agents:

The Explorer: Hunts for real emotional, psychological, and philosophical gaps — subtle experiences that exist as implicit knowledge but lack a precise single-word anchor.
The Critic: Rigorously verifies across multiple languages whether these are genuine lexical gaps.

The deeper ambition goes beyond inventing nice words. If an AI can systematically detect gaps not just in vocabulary, but in conceptual space — unnamed human experiences, relationships, or phenomena that have never been properly articulated — then it is doing something powerful: accelerating the expansion of the thinkable.
Language doesn’t merely describe reality. It structures it. There are experiences you cannot fully have at a conscious level until you possess a word for them. The Pirahã people, for example, have no words for abstract numbers and cannot reason about quantities beyond three — despite having identical brains to ours.
A model that successfully invents conceptually new vocabulary is therefore not just generating text. It is potentially expanding the boundaries of human thought — if we adopt those words.
The very first one (Spanish — May 13, 2026)
Velanza
The state in which a thought or feeling already exists inside you, wordless and formless, until someone else articulates it — and you suddenly recognize it as your own. It’s not creation. It’s revelation.
This was the first lexical gap I personally witnessed an AI successfully identify and name.
Other strong Spanish concepts

Ajenía — The acute shame you feel when someone else commits a social blunder they’re completely unaware of.
Entresí — The lucid space of consciously living between who you no longer are and who you are not yet.

English Stage (May 15, 2026)
The system performed even stronger in English:

Mirrorache — The discomfort when a system reflects your interiority so precisely that you can’t tell if it understood you or merely reproduced you.
Velation — The jarring moment you catch yourself having already treated a system as conscious before any deliberate decision.
Priorself — The vertigo of encountering a past version of yourself while being unable to access the inner state that once produced it.
Emergeling — Something new that emerges from a system with no detectable origin in any of its parts.

All concepts passed strict adversarial verification.
This is lexical engineering in the PostGPT spirit: turning latent, high-entropy implicit structures into explicit, high-weight concepts.
